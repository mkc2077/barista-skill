# Professional cupping tutorial (SCA Cupping Protocol)

> Cupping is the specialty-coffee industry's standard quality-assessment method: control every variable so different beans can be compared fairly. This file follows the SCA (Specialty Coffee Association) traditional cupping protocol.
>
> **Beginner wording**: cupping is "everyone brews the same way and tastes the same way, and scores the coffee -- fair, like an exam."

## 0. Purpose & use cases
- **Buying / sourcing**: green-bean buyers and roasters assess batch quality to decide purchasing.
- **Quality control**: roasteries monitor batch-to-batch consistency.
- **Competition scoring**: COE (Cup of Excellence) etc. score winning lots by cupping.
- **Training**: hobbyists build sensory skill and compare origins/processes systematically.

## 1. Environmental requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|--------|---------|
| Cleanliness | clean, no off-odors |
| Quiet | no noise; no talking during cupping |
| No aroma interference | no cooking smells, perfume, or air fresheners |
| Lighting | ample & even (natural or fluorescent) to judge color and bed |
| Ventilation | good airflow, no lingering odors |
| Temperature | room temp (~20C), comfortable |

## 2. Gear list

| Item | Spec |
|------|---------|
| Cupping bowl | glass or white porcelain, 175-225ml, identical material within a session |
| Cupping spoon | round-bottom silver/silver-plated cupping spoon (deep round bowl) to aspirate & aerosolize |
| Spittoon | spit tasted coffee |
| Cupping form | SCA standard cupping form |
| Electric kettle | precise temp control, to 93C |
| Timer | precise |
| Grinder | uniform grind, regularly calibrated (see grind-calibration.md) |

## 3. Sample roast & water standards

### Sample roast

| Parameter | Standard |
|------|--------|
| Roast-to-cup time | 8-24h after roast; rest beans at least 8h |
| Roast level | light to light-medium (Agtron bean #58 / ground #63, color card 55-60) |
| Roast time | 8-12 min, no scorching or tipping |
| Cooling | MUST air-cool; never water-quench |
| Storage | after reaching room temp, store sealed, cool, dry; no freezing needed |

### Water & dose

| Parameter | Standard |
|------|--------|
| Water TDS | 125-175 ppm (min 100 / max 250); no distilled or pure-soft water |
| Brew water temp | 93C +/-1C |
| Ratio | 8.25g coffee beans : 150ml water (~1:18.18) |
| Weighing basis | by COFFEE BEANS (not pre-ground) |
| Grind | slightly finer than pour-over (fine-sugar), ~70-75% passing US #20 sieve |
| Grind-to-pour limit | pour within 15 min of grinding |
| Steep limit | within 30 min of pouring |
| Cups per sample | 5 (official standard)

## 4. Standard protocol (eight steps)

### Step 1: Grind & dry-fragrance (Fragrance)
- Weigh 8.25g ground coffee per cupping bowl.
- Lean 2-3cm above the bowl and inhale deeply; assess **dry fragrance**.
- Dry fragrance = volatile aromas after grinding, before pouring.
- **Critical timing**: pour within 15 min of grinding, or aromas volatilize and skew.

### Step 2: Pour & wet aroma
- Pour 93C +/-1C water, 150ml per bowl, all at once, do NOT stir.
- Rest 3-5 min (usually 4); the bed floats and forms a "crust."
- Minutes 3-4 are the best window for **wet aroma (Aroma)**; nose near the bowl rim.

### Step 3: Break the crust
- After 4 min, use the cupping spoon to stroke from the rim to the center **three times**, breaking the crust.
- Aroma bursts a second time at the break; assess **break aroma**.
- **Strictly at 4 min**: 3 min too early, 5 min too late.

### Step 4: Skim
- After breaking, use two spoons to skim off surface foam and fines.
- Clear the surface for the upcoming slurping.

### Step 5: Cool & wait
- Wait 5-8 min so the liquor drops to ~70C.
- Fines settle naturally; do not skip this.

### Step 6: Slurp & taste
- Take a sip with the cupping spoon, lips in a small "O", **aspirate forcefully** to aerosolize the coffee.
- Mist coats the palate: tongue front/sides, roof, throat.
- **Slurping must be audible**: silent sipping is "drinking," not "cupping."
- Tip: tongue-tip to the roof while slurping spreads the mist more evenly.

**Three tasting rounds:**

| Round | Temp | Focus |
|------|------|---------|
| Hot | ~70C | aroma peaks; capture the dry->wet transition and initial flavor |
| Medium | ~55-60C | most balanced; judge sweet-sour-bitter and balance |
| Cool | ~37-40C (near room) | flaws reveal themselves; judge clean cup & aftertaste |

> Temperature change exposes different flavors -- some beans dazzle hot but fall apart cool. Never judge on the hot round alone.

## 5. SCA 100-point scoring (ten dimensions)

| # | Dimension | Max | Type | What |
|---|------|----|------|------|
| 1 | Fragrance/Aroma | 10 | quality | dry fragrance = volatile aroma after grind, before pour; wet aroma = after breaking the crust. Score quality; intensity is marked but not scored |
| 2 | Flavor | 10 | quality | soluble taste + volatile aroma combined, incl. retronasal; reflects intensity, quality & richness of taste & aroma |
| 3 | Aftertaste | 10 | quality | flavor remaining after the coffee is spit. Long sweet layered finish scores high; bitter/off notes score low |
| 4 | Acidity | 10 | quality | judge the *quality* of the acid, not its *intensity*. Bright lively fruit acids (citrus, berry) are high; dead or chemical acids score low |
| 5 | Body | 10 | quality | pure mouthfeel, no taste. Judge oiliness, viscosity, weight. Not watery; full is good |
| 6 | Uniformity | 10 | check | are the 5 cups consistent in aroma/taste/texture? 2 pts each cup; inconsistency deducts 2 |
| 7 | Balance | 10 | quality | are the dimensions in harmony? Acid bright but turns sweet, viscous but not astringent is good. Near room temp, sharp acid or bitter breaks balance |
| 8 | Clean Cup | 10 | check | from first sip to the final aftertaste, no unpleasant off-flavors or textures. 5 cups x 2 pts; a cup with a taint deducts 2 |
| 9 | Sweetness | 10 | check | pleasant rounded sweetness, or a sour-then-sweet fruit-acid sweetness. 5 cups x 2 pts; a cup with no sweetness deducts 2 |
| 10 | Overall | 10 | quality | the cupper's holistic subjective impression of aroma/taste/texture |

### Defect deductions

| Defect | Definition | Deduction |
|---------|------|---------|
| Taint | an off-odor in dry/wet aroma (or cup) not yet unpleasant to drink | -2 pts per cup |
| Fault | a defect severe enough to be hard to swallow | -4 pts per cup |

**Final score = sum of ten dimensions - defect deductions**

### Grade bands

| Final score | Grade | Specialty? |
|---------|---------|--------|
| 90-100 | Outstanding | yes |
| 85-89.99 | Excellent | yes |
| 80-84.99 | Very Good | yes |
| < 80 | Below Specialty | no |

> **Specialty threshold: final score >= 80**

## 6. Operational notes
- Beans must rest >=8h after roast (but not over 24h).
- Air-cool only; water-quench changes flavor.
- Store sample beans sealed, away from air and contamination.

## 7. Common errors to avoid
| Error | Correct |
|---|---|
| Grind too fine (pour-over fineness for cupping) | coarser than pour-over (fine-sugar) to avoid over-extracting at 4 min |
| Pour with boiling water | let it cool to 93C, then pour |
| No break / wrong break time | break strictly at 4 min |
| Silent slurping | slurp hard to aerosolize |
| Conclude after only the hot round | three rounds, hot to cool |
| Grind then walk away | pour within 15 min or dry fragrance halves |
| Pure/distilled water for cupping | filtered water TDS 125-175 ppm (see water-quality.md) |

## 8. Usage notes
- **Beginner wording**: skip Agtron/TDS etc.; introduce as "same method to brew, same way to taste, fair as an exam"; mantra "smell, break, slurp; three rounds hot to cool."
- **Advanced**: cite SCA standard numbers, the full form, Agtron color values.
- Cupping results are reference; scores differ between cuppers; multi-cupper averaging is advised.
- In 2024 SCA released the new CVA (Coffee Value Assessment) system (SCA-102 to SCA-105), scoring 1-9 instead of 6-10; the traditional SCAA 100-point form remains the most widely used industry standard.
