# Parameter tuning guide

> This file systematically covers the core principles of coffee extraction and how to tune scientifically for different beans, roast levels, and personal taste.
>
> **Beginner wording**: think of brewing as a "mixing desk" — ratio sets strength, temp sets speed, time sets depth, grind sets coarseness; turn ONE knob at a time.

## 0. SCA Golden Cup Standard

Long-standing extraction guidance from SCAA (Specialty Coffee Association of America) and SCAE (Europe):

| Parameter | SCAA standard | SCAE standard |
|------|---------|---------|
| Extraction Yield | 18%-22% | 18%-22% |
| TDS (strength) | 1.15%-1.35% | ~1.3% |
| Brew ratio | 1:15-1:20 (usually 1:18) | 50-65g coffee / 1L water |
| Water temp | 92-96C | 92-96C |
| Contact time | ~3 min (immersion) | - |

> SCAE Golden Cup definition: "1 liter of fresh water at 92-96C passes through 50-65g of freshly ground coffee, filtered through a bleached paper filter, extracting 18%-22% of the coffee solids."

### Reading the golden-cup band

- **Extraction < 18%**: under-extracted — sour/astringent, little sweetness, possibly salty, short finish
- **18%-22%**: golden band — sweet & mature, clear transparent flavor, balanced sweet-sour, long finish
- **> 22%**: over-extracted — strong bitter/astringent, dry/puckering, hollow & dull

## 1. Core parameters and their principles

### 1.1 Brew ratio
- The core lever for TDS (strength) and extraction yield.
- Higher ratio (more grounds, less water) -> higher strength, lower extraction yield.
- Pour-over standard 1:15-1:20; espresso 1:1.8-1:2.5.
- Beginner mantra: "weak -> more grounds less water, strong -> more water less grounds."

### 1.2 Water temperature
- Ideal 91-96C (just below boil). Higher temp extracts faster; too low and some compounds won't dissolve.
- This is why cold brew needs much more time and tastes softer.
- Mantra add-on: "sour -> raise temp, bitter -> lower temp."

### 1.3 Extraction time
- Pour-over usually 2-4 min; espresso 25-30s. Longer = more extraction, but too long = bitter over-extraction.

### 1.4 Flow rate
- Pouring too fast or irregularly causes channeling. Water takes the path of least resistance, so grounds-bed uniformity is critical.
- Mantra: "fast flow -> finer, slow flow -> coarser."

### 1.5 Dissolution order (core science)
Soluble compounds in roasted coffee dissolve in a fixed order:
1. **Fruit acids** (first) — sour, some sweet
2. **Lipids** — body
3. **Sugars** — sweet, body
4. **Carbohydrates** (last) — body, bitterness

> Acids and salts dissolve more easily than sugars; this is why under-extracted coffee leans sour/salty — sugars haven't had time to fully dissolve.

## 2. Tuning by bean character

### 2.1 By origin

| Origin | Flavor | Suggested temp | Suggested ratio | Grind lean |
|------|---------|---------|----------|----------|
| Ethiopia (Yirgacheffe etc.) | floral, citrus, berry, tea-like, bright acid | 92-94C (light) | 1:16-1:17 | medium-fine, slightly finer to lift florals |
| Kenya (SL28/SL34) | bold berry, tomato, savory, full body | 93-95C | 1:15-1:16 | medium-fine |
| Colombia | balanced, caramel, nut, mild acidity | 90-93C | 1:15-1:16 | medium |
| Brazil | nut, chocolate, low acidity, full body | 88-91C | 1:14-1:15 | medium-coarse |
| Panama (Geisha) | jasmine, bergamot, berry, elegant/delicate | 92-94C (light) | 1:16-1:18 | medium-fine (C40 ~24) |
| Yunnan | balanced, brown sugar, nut, mild | 90-93C | 1:15-1:16 | medium |

### 2.2 By variety

| Variety | Flavor | Extraction notes |
|------|---------|---------|
| Geisha | strong floral, delicate | don't over-extract and bury florals; slightly higher temp (92-94C), medium-fine, 1:16-1:18 |
| Typica | nut, chocolate, herbal, soft acidity | medium params suit |
| Bourbon | cocoa, orange, caramel, chocolate, sweet-forward | can extend extraction to lift sweetness |
| SL28/SL34 (Kenya) | malt, caramel, citrus, berry, full | needs full extraction to show berry |
| Pacamara | complex, floral & fruity | needs fine tuning |
| Catuai | apple, peach, chocolate, balanced | universal params work |

### 2.3 By process

| Process | Flavor | Tuning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Washed | clean, clear, bright acidity | lower solubility; slightly higher temp or finer; ratio a touch higher (1:16-1:17) |
| Natural | fruity, sweet, full body, funky | higher solubility (mucilage residue); slightly lower temp or coarser to avoid over-extraction; ratio lower (1:15-1:16) |
| Honey | between washed & natural; sweet-forward, balanced | medium params, tune by honey level (white/yellow/red/black) |
| Anaerobic | intense, unique, funky, wine-like | solubility varies with fermentation; often lower temp (88-92C) to avoid pulling too much funk |

### 2.4 By roast level (most important dimension)

| Roast | Solubility | Temp | Grind | Time | Principle |
|--------|--------|---------|--------|---------|------|
| Light | low | 93-96C | finer | longer | dense structure needs more energy; bright acids & florals need full extraction |
| Medium | medium | 90-93C | medium | standard | balanced, near golden-cup |
| Dark | high | 88-91C | coarser | shorter | long roast = porous, easy to extract; fast pull = bitter |

**Core principle**: dark roast is more porous from longer heat, so solubles dissolve faster. Switching light -> dark, consider a coarser grind.
**Beginner mantra**: dark roast -> coarser & lower temp; light roast -> finer & higher temp.

## 3. Tuning by personal taste

| Taste goal | Direction | Mantra |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Highlight acidity | coarser / lower temp / shorter / lower ratio | to cut acidity go finer (i.e. less extraction surfaces)... |
| Boost sweetness | land in the sugar-dissolution window / slightly higher temp / slightly longer | - |
| Reduce bitterness | coarser / lower temp / shorter / higher ratio | bitter -> coarser |
| Add body | lower ratio (stronger) / slightly finer | strong -> more water less grounds (reverse) |
| Flavor clarity | better extraction uniformity / mid ratio | - |

## 4. Worked examples

### Example 1: light-roast Ethiopia natural, pour-over
- Start: C40 22 / 92C / 1:16 / 2:45
- Feedback: sharp acidity, lacks sweetness
- Analysis: light = dense, 92C may under-extract sugars; natural's mucilage may clog.
- Tune (ONE variable first): raise temp to 94C (sour->raise). Taste; if sweetness up, lock it. If still sour, grind +1 finer (C40 21). Goal: give sugars enough energy to balance the sharp acid.

### Example 2: dark-roast Brazil espresso
- Start: 18g / 36g / 92C / 28s
- Feedback: heavy burnt, short finish
- Analysis: dark = high solubility, 92C too hot -> over-extraction, carbohydrates over-pulled.
- Tune: drop temp to 90C (bitter->lower). Taste; if burnt eases, lock it. If still bitter, grind +0.5 coarser. Dark roast -> coarser & lower temp — mantra matches the science.

### Example 3: medium Colombia pour-over too weak
- Start: 15g / 240g / 90C / 1:16 / 3:00
- Feedback: watery/weak
- Analysis: medium at 90C is low, under-extracted; 1:16 may be thin.
- Tune: ratio 1:16 -> 1:15 (15g/225g). Taste; if stronger but still flat, raise temp to 92C.

## 5. Variable priority & tuning order
When several params may need tuning, go in this priority:
1. **Grind** (first lever) — biggest effect, fastest result
2. **Temp** (second lever) — when grind is maxed; +/−1-2C
3. **Ratio** — adjusts strength
4. **Time** — indirectly controlled via grind

> **Iron rule (see SKILL.md)**: change ONE variable at a time, taste before the next.
