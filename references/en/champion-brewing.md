# Champion brewing recipes index

> Iron rule: This file only provides "verified champion name + competition title + core method name + dripper/filter index." Any specific number (dose/temp/ratio/time) MUST be re-verified online against the champion's current public recipe before you write it into a reply. Always append a source link + retrieval date.
> Champions iterate methods year by year; competition recipes differ from home recipes. Always verify before advising.

## 1. SCA brewing & Golden Cup (calibrate first)

- **Golden Cup**: extraction yield 18–22%, TDS 1.15–1.35%, brew ratio 1:18–1:19 (pour-over often wider, 1:14–1:17). See [parameters-guide.md](parameters-guide.md) section 1.
- **WBrC (World Brewers Cup)**: two services — Open Service (competitor's own beans + chosen recipe) and Compulsory Service (organizer-supplied beans + blind brew). Scored on flavor, technique, and presentation.
- **Resting (degassing)**: light roast 7–15 days, medium-dark 5–10 days (general range; verify per bean with roaster).

## 2. Drippers & filter paper index

> Dripper geometry sets flow rate and extraction shape; cup AND paper choice are the first variables in pour-over. The table below is verified; fix the user's dripper first, then layer champion technique.

### 2.1 Mainstream drippers characteristics

| Dripper | Geometry | Flow | Extraction shape | Best for | Filter |
|---------|----------|------|------------------|----------|--------|
| **Hario V60** | 60° cone, internal spiral ribs, one large hole at bottom | Fast | Through-flow, high rate; the grounds hold the water back | Light-medium roast, floral/fruity, bright acidity | V60 conical paper (01/02) |
| **V60 Kasuya Model** (Kasuya signature) | 60° cone, **bottom spiral ribs removed** -> slower flow | Medium-slow | For coarse-grind 4:6, flow deliberately slowed to boost strength | 4:6 method, coarse grind | V60 conical paper |
| **Origami** (folding cup) | Folded cone, 20 vertical ridges; **one cup, two geometries** | Adjustable | Cone paper -> V60 style (bright acidity/aroma); Wave paper -> rounder sweetness/body | Competition favorite; flavor play | V60 cone paper OR Kalita wave paper |
| **Origami Air** (resin) | Same Origami geometry, open-hole resin, more airflow | Fast-ish | Fast drawdown, forgiving | 2023 WBrC champion's cup | Same as Origami |
| **Kalita Wave** 155/185 | Flat bottom + 3 small holes | Slow & stable | More immersion feel, even extraction, forgiving | Beginners, medium-dark, stability | Kalita wave paper |
| **Chemex** | Cone, thick paper (~20–30 um) | Slow | Very clean, near zero fines; oil filtered out | Larger batches, clean profile | Chemex proprietary thick paper |
| **Clever / Abid dripper** | Flat + immersion valve | Controllable (immersion) | Immersion extraction, stable, forgiving | Used in competition too; stable beginner method | Cake/cone paper |
| **Metal filter** (e.g. metal V60) | Same as V60 but metal mesh | Fast | Oils and fines kept, heavy body | Rich flavor but fines | None (metal mesh) |

### 2.2 Filter paper shape effect on flavor (verified tests)

> Same bean, same grind, same temp, only swap paper (verified by Roomie / sspai tests):

| Paper shape | Verified flavor direction | Flow | Best for |
|-------------|--------------------------|------|----------|
| **Conical (V60 type)** | Brighter acidity, floral/fruity to the front, clean juice-like body | Faster | Light roast, want "juice feel" |
| **Wave (Kalita type)** | Rounder sweetness, heavier body, mellower acidity, more even extraction | Slower | Medium-dark roast, want "round body" |
| **Thick (Chemex)** | Extremely clean, near-zero fines, soft acidity | Slow | Want "ultra-clean" larger batch |

**Key takeaway**: Origami's value is that you switch from "bright acidity" to "round sweetness" by only swapping the paper — no second dripper needed. That's why recent WBrC competitors keep choosing Origami (one cup, two geometries).

## 3. Champion brewing index (online search starting points)

> The table below is a search starting point; before advising, re-query that champion's current recipe by machine / bean / roast.

### 1. Tetsu Kasuya — the 4:6 method
- Title: 2016 WBrC World Champion (first Asian pour-over world champion).
- Dripper: Hario V60 (signature Kasuya Model removes bottom ribs, slowing flow for 4:6 coarse grind).
- Core: split total water into **front 40% (controls acidity/sweetness) + back 60% (controls strength/body)**.
- Official baseline (verified via HARIO interview; still re-verify): 20g dose / 300ml total / 1:15 / 92C / 3:30 total.
  - Pour 1 @ 0:00 -> 50ml (front-40% 42%, targeting sweetness)
  - Pour 2 @ 0:45 -> 70ml (completes front 40% = 120ml)
  - Pour 3 @ 1:30 -> 60ml (back-60% segment 1)
  - Pour 4 @ 2:10 -> 60ml (back-60% segment 2)
  - Pour 5 @ 2:40 -> 60ml (back-60% segment 3, totaling 180ml)
  - Remove V60 at 3:30.
- Tuning logic (verified points):
  - Too sour/sharp -> reduce front-40% pulses (3->2 or 2->1) for more concentrated extraction and earlier sweetness.
  - Too thin/weak -> make back-60% more concentrated (fewer pulses, larger pours) for faster strength build-up.
  - Overall strength -> move brew ratio first (1:16 brighter, 1:14 fuller).
  - Change one variable at a time; order: pulses -> ratio -> temp/grind.
- Book: "The Simple, Powerful Way to Brew Delicious Coffee: 4:6 Method" (2023).
- Search: `Tetsu Kasuya 4:6 method` / `Kasuya V60 Kasuya model`.

### 2. Chad Wang — VWI by CHADWANG
- Title: 2017 WBrC World Champion.

### 4. Berg Wu — "Three-temperature" pour-over (san-yu-wen)
- Title: 2016 WBC World Barista Champion; Simple Kaffa co-founder.
- Dripper: Clever dripper (immersion-style).
- Method: three different water temperatures across one brew.
- Public demo points (circulated version; verify before use): Ethiopian washed / 16g / 240g; Clever dripper; 30s bloom; first stage near-boiling water, add cold water to drop to ~88–93C as it nears dripping; second stage pour to 200g; remove lower pot, add cold water to ~80–85C; final 40g steep 20s. Warmer drop = brighter acidity; cooler drop = heavier sweetness.
- Search: `Berg Wu three temperature pour over` / `Simple Kaffa brew`.

## 7. Common pitfalls

- Don't copy competition recipes directly — competition beans, grinder, water differ from home; run a baseline comparison.
- Don't remember the name but forget the version — the same person's recipe changes a lot year to year; always search with the year.
- Don't treat "world-changing" methods as unchanging truth — 4:6 and three-temperature are adjustable frameworks, not the only solution.
- Don't fabricate numbers from a name — every dose/temp/ratio must come from online verification.
- Don't copy pours across drippers — the same V60 pour flows totally differently in Origami wave paper; treat "dripper + paper" as the first variable when tuning.
- Do fix the dripper, build a baseline, then layer champion pulses — change one variable.
