# Stable starter parameters (beginner-proof)

Starter recipes that "won't ruin your first cup." Covers 14 common brew methods. Advanced users tune from here.
All recipes assume fresh beans (best 2-4 weeks off roast) and a scale.

> For bean selection and how roast/process/freshness affect extraction, see `beans.md`.

**Universal mantra (memorize):**
> bitter->grind coarser, sour->grind finer; weak->more grounds less water, strong->more water less grounds; fast flow->finer, slow flow->coarser.
> (Full mantra rules and beginner usage see the skill's beginner constraints.)

## 1. Espresso

For: semi-auto machine + grinder (adjustable)

- Dose: 18g (double)
- Yield: ~36g (ratio ~1:2)
- Temp: 92-94C
- Time: 25-30s
- Grind: medium-fine, tuned to hit 36g in 25-30s
- Steps:
  1. Weigh 18g into the portafilter, level.
  2. Tamp level (light tamp, no heavy force).
  3. Start and time simultaneously.
  4. Stop at ~36g (~27s).
- Too bitter -> grind coarser (bitter->coarser); too sour -> grind finer (sour->finer)

### 1-B. Espresso variants: single / Ristretto / Lungo

#### Single shot
- Dose: 7-9g (7g basket or single portafilter)
- Yield: 14-18g (~1:2)
- Time: 20-28s
- For: one quick cup. Mind single-basket capacity; don't force 14g.

#### Ristretto (short, intense)
- Dose: 18g (double basket)
- Yield: 18-27g (1:1-1:1.5, shorter than standard espresso)
- Time: 15-22s
- For: flat white, dirty, signature bases; sweeter, more concentrated.
- Grind a bit finer to compensate for the shorter time; re-tune if bitter/sharp.

#### Lungo (long)
- Dose: 18g (double basket)
- Yield: 54-72g (1:3-1:4, longer than standard)
- Time: 35-45s
- For: americano, or a larger undiluted cup.
- Grind a bit coarser to avoid over-extracted bitterness.

#### Americano
- Hot: espresso (18g/36g) + hot water 150-200ml, ratio 1:4-1:6 to taste.
- Iced: espresso 36g + ice + cold water 200ml.
- Advanced: water-first keeps crema; espresso-first is more even. Both are fine.

### 1-C. Superautomatic / capsule users
For a superauto (one-touch, fixed dose & grind) or capsule machine (Nespresso etc.), "grind/temp tuning" doesn't apply — the machine decides. Your levers:
- **Bean/capsule choice**: milk drinks -> medium-dark blend capsule; single-origin flavor -> light SOE capsule.
- **Cup button**: espresso (small, strong) vs lungo; milk drinks start from espresso.
- **Milk ratio**: latte more milk, flat white less milk/thin foam, dirty un-stirred.
- **Taste tweak**: same capsule differs slightly across machines; if too bitter, try a lighter roast capsule or add milk/water.
> Superauto/capsule focus is "pick the right bean + tune milk ratio," not grind; return to the semi-auto path if you need fresh-grind tuning.

## 2. Pour-over

For: gooseneck kettle + dripper (e.g. V60) + grinder + scale

- Dose: 15g
- Water: 240g (~1:16)
- Temp: 90-92C (dark 88, light 93)
- Grind: medium (raw sugar)
- Total time: 2:30-3:30
- Steps (pulsed pouring):
  1. Add grounds, bloom with 30g water 30s.
  2. Pour in 2-3 slow stages to 240g, circle on the grounds, avoid the paper edge.
  3. Let it drip through.
- Too bitter/burnt -> grind coarser (bitter->coarser); too sour/weak -> grind finer (sour->finer)

### 2-B. Kalita Wave (beginner-friendlier)
For: Kalita Wave 155 (1-2 cups) / 185 (2-3) + Kalita filter + kettle + grinder
- Dose: 15g (155) / 20g (185); Water: 240g / 320g (~1:16)
- Temp: 90-92C (dark 88, light 93); Grind: medium (a touch finer than V60 since Kalita drains slower)
- Total time: 2:30-3:30
- Steps:
  1. Rinse filter (removes paper taste, seats it), discard the water below.
  2. Bloom 30-40g for 30-45s (Kalita drains slower, bloom a bit longer).
  3. Pour in 2-3 slow stages, small circles from center (~1cm), avoid the edge.
  4. Let it drip (usually within 3 min).

## 3. French Press

For: French press + grinder + scale

- Dose: 15g; Water: 250g; Temp: 93C
- Time: steep 4 min; Grind: coarse (sea salt)
- Steps: mix grounds & water -> steep 4 min -> press down -> pour
- Too bitter -> grind coarser; too sour -> finer or steep longer

## 4. AeroPress

For: AeroPress + paper/metal filter + grinder

- Dose: 15-17g; Yield: 220-250g
- Temp: 80-90C (dark lower, light higher)
- Time: steep 1 min + press 20-30s
- Grind: medium-fine (finer than pour-over, coarser than espresso)
- Steps: add grounds & water -> stir twice -> steep 1 min -> insert plunger & press slowly (20-30s)
- Too bitter -> grind coarser or lower temp; too sour -> finer or raise temp

## 5. Moka Pot

For: moka pot + grinder + stove

- Dose: 18-20g; Water: cold water to just below the safety valve
- Heat: medium-low; Time: 1-2 min of coffee flow
- Grind: medium-fine (fine sugar)
- Steps: cold water in lower -> fill basket level, no tamping -> tighten -> medium heat -> switch to low once flowing -> cut heat at the gurgle
- Too bitter -> lower heat / grind coarser / don't let it gurgle long; too sour -> -

## 6. Cold Brew

For: lidded jar/bottle + strainer/bag + fridge

- Dose: 50-70g; Water: 500-700g
- Temp: room temp or cold; Time: fridge 12-24h; Grind: coarse (sea salt)
- Steps: stir grounds & water -> cover & refrigerate -> strain when done
- Too bitter -> coarser or shorter; too sour -> finer or longer

## 7. Ice Drip / Cold Drip

For: ice drip tower/brewer + ice + filter

- Dose: 40-60g; Water: 400-600g ice water
- Time: 4-8h (1 drop/sec); Grind: medium-coarse
- Steps: level grounds -> adjust valve for slow ice-water drip -> collect -> chill & drink
- Too bitter -> coarser / more water; too sour -> slower valve / finer

## 8. Clever Dripper

For: Clever Dripper + filter + scale + grinder

- Dose: 15-20g; Water: 240-300g; Temp: 90-93C
- Time: steep 2-3 min; Grind: medium (raw sugar)
- Steps: insert filter & grounds -> pour to target -> cover & steep 2-3 min -> set on cup to drain
- Too bitter -> coarser; too sour -> finer

## 9. Signature, classic milk drinks & iced pour-over

Before starting a signature drink, classic milk drink, or iced pour-over, list the recipe and gear first. Safe starters below.

> Beginner one-liner for milk drinks: more milk = milder; thicker foam = more "airy." Want intense -> flat white/cortado; want smooth -> latte; want thick foam in a small cup -> cappuccino.

### Classic espresso-milk ratio cheat sheet (double shot ~36-40g base)

| Drink | Espresso | Steamed milk | Foam | Volume | Character |
|------|----------|--------------|------|--------|-----------|
| Espresso Macchiato | 1 shot | tiny (a spoon) | a dab | 60-80ml | espresso-led, milk just "marks" it |
| Cortado | 1 shot | equal warm milk (~40-60g) | almost none | 90-120ml | 1:1, most balanced |
| Flat White | 1 shot (often ristretto) | ~100-150ml microfoam | very thin (<=0.5cm) | 150-160ml | espresso-forward, velvety |
| Cappuccino | 1 shot | equal steamed milk (~60ml) | thick (1-2cm) | 150-180ml | 1:1:1 layered; thick foam is the soul |
| Caffe Latte | 1 shot | lots of milk (~150-240ml) | thin (0.5-1cm) | 220-300ml | milk-led, smoothest |
| Mocha | 1 shot | steamed milk + chocolate | thin + chocolate | 200-250ml | latte + chocolate, sweet |
| Con Panna | 1 shot | - | - | 40-60ml | espresso + whipped cream dollop |
| Americano | 1 shot | - (add hot water) | - | 150-250ml | see "Americano" above |

> "1 shot" = double basket yielding 36-40g (~1:2); single shot ~25-30g. Cups vary; ratios are relative. Cross-checked online 2026-07-15 (expertcafe / completehomebarista / coffeebros / coffee-guide.jp).

### Iced pour-over
- Gear: dripper (V60) + matching filter + kettle + iced cup + ice + grounds + hot water.
- Dose 15g / ice ~100g / hot water ~150g (~1:16-1:17, ice counts as part of water) / temp 90-93C (light can go higher) / grind medium-fine.
- Steps: bloom 30s -> pour in stages to target -> drip directly onto ice.
- Light florals shine as iced pour-over; dark roasts tend to read bitter, avoid.

### Signature drink general points
- **Pick the extraction first**: espresso (1:2) or ristretto (1:1-1:1.5, shorter, stronger)? Flat white/dirty often use ristretto or standard espresso.
- **Bean choice**: milk bases (latte/flat white/dirty/cappuccino) -> medium-dark blend; to showcase the bean (SOE flat white, signature) -> light SOE.
- **Basket & filter**: respect basket capacity (18g/20g/22g), don't over-fill; signature work needs the right basket/filter, prep them first.

### Per-drink methods (ratios cross-checked online)

#### Cappuccino (1:1:1 layered)
- Ratio: 1 double shot (36-40g) + steamed milk ~equal (~60ml) + thick foam (1-2cm); volume 150-180ml in a 5-6oz cup.
- Bean: medium-dark blend.
- Steps: pre-warm cup -> pull shot -> steam milk (aerate 3-5s, longer than latte, swirl to 60-65C) -> pour milk to combine, spoon foam ~1-2cm on top -> optional cocoa/cinnamon.
- Mantra: cappuccino = thick foam, small cup; more milk -> latte; more intense -> flat white/cortado.
- Verified: traditional 1:1:1, cup 150-180ml, foam >=2cm.

#### Caffe Latte
- Ratio: 1 shot + lots of milk (~150-240ml) + thin foam (0.5-1cm); volume 220-300ml, large cup.
- Steps: pull shot -> steam milk (brief AerATE, mostly whirlpool, thin foam) -> milk first, thin foam finish -> latte art optional.
- vs cappuccino: more milk, bigger cup, thinner foam; vs flat white: more milk.

#### Flat White
- Ratio: 1 shot (often ristretto for strength) + microfoam ~100-150ml, foam very thin (<=0.5cm, integrated); volume 150-160ml.
- Steps: pull ristretto -> steam "velvet" microfoam (almost no distinct foam layer) -> pour; surface is a flat layer of fine bubbles.
- Note: it's integrated microfoam, not thick foam; don't make cappuccino foam.

#### Cortado
- Ratio: 1 shot + equal warm milk (~40-60g), almost no foam; volume 90-120ml.
- Bean: medium-dark.
- Steps: pull shot -> add equal gently warmed/lightly-aerated milk -> combine. Small glass.
- Character: balanced; milk only "lowers the intensity," doesn't take over.

#### Macchiato (two opposite versions — confirm first)
- **Espresso Macchiato**: 1 shot + a small spoon of milk/a dab of foam; volume 60-80ml. Espresso-led.
- **Latte Macchiato**: reverse — pour lots of steamed milk (~200ml) in a tall glass, then slowly pour 1 shot into the middle, making three layers (milk/coffee/milk); ratio ~1:6, milk-led.
- Note: the two "macchiato" are opposites; confirm which before advising.

#### Mocha (latte + chocolate)
- Ratio: 1 shot + chocolate 15-30g + hot milk ~60-80ml + thin foam; topped with whipped cream/chocolate. Volume 200-250ml.
- Steps: chocolate in cup -> add & stir hot milk -> pour espresso -> thin foam -> optional whipped cream.
- Bean: medium-dark blend pairs best.

#### Espresso Con Panna
- Ratio: 1 shot (~36g) + a dollop of whipped cream (~20-40ml) on top; volume 40-60ml.
- Steps: pull shot into small cup -> whip cream just pourable -> float on coffee -> optional cocoa.
- Note: don't whip too stiff; some call Vienna coffee "con panna," confirm before making.

#### Irish Coffee (alcoholic sweet drink)
- Ingredients: hot coffee/double shot + water (~150ml) + Irish whiskey 30-40ml + brown sugar 1 tsp + whipped cream (floated).
- Steps: warm glass -> stir sugar into hot coffee -> add whiskey -> float cream off the back of a spoon (do not stir).
- Note: contains alcohol; confirm if a non-alcohol version is wanted.

#### Vienna Coffee
- Ratio: 1-2 shots + hot water ~100ml (small americano) + lots of whipped cream on top + chocolate shavings.
- Steps: make small americano -> pour -> two spoons whipped cream on top -> chocolate shavings.
- Difference: con panna is "espresso + thin cream"; Vienna is "coffee + thick cream top + chocolate," more dessert-like.

### Affogato (coffee + ice cream, dessert-tier)
- Ingredients: vanilla gelato 1-2 scoops (50-100g, denser is better; fluffy cheap ice cream melts into soup) + a fresh shot (25-40ml; double is stronger). Optional: ristretto (sweeter/thicker), a splash of Amaretto/Frangelico.
- Steps: chill the cup 10-15 min -> scoop gelato -> pour a FRESH shot straight on top -> serve at once with a small spoon, do NOT stir; enjoy the hot/cold contrast for a few seconds.
- Bean: medium-dark (chocolate/caramel/nut) pairs best; light roast's sharp acid clashes with vanilla.
- Note: it's a dessert, not a drink; the shot must be fresh-poured.
- Verified: classic 1 scoop gelato (50-100g) + 1 hot shot (25-40ml), ristretto is better, serve immediately.

### Three easy signature examples (with gear)
- **Coconut latte**: shot 36g + iced coconut drink 150g + milk 100g, stir with ice; gear: espresso machine, 18g basket, milk jug.
- **Lemon americano**: shot 36g + sparkling water 150g + a little lemon juice (add a little, taste, then more) + ice; gear: machine, iced cup.
- **Coconut-cloud latte** (Luckin-style): shot 36g + coconut cream (thick coconut milk) 80-120g + milk 80g foamed (milk frother or steam wand) into a "cloud" + pour shot on top; gear: machine, frother, glass.

### Hot foam vs cold foam
- **Hot foam** (latte/cappuccino/flat white base): cold milk 5-8C start, wand just below surface 1-2cm -> hiss (aerate) 3-5s to expand volume -> sink & whirl to heat -> target 55-65C, over 70C proteins denature -> thickness: flat white thin microfoam (0.5-1cm) / cappuccino thick (1-2cm) / latte thin.
- **Cold foam / iced latte**: a few ice cubes + milk 150ml in a jug, froth 5-10s (manual/hand frother) -> pour into an ice-filled cup -> add espresso 36g -> do NOT steam cold milk (volume is small and it heats instantly, won't foam) -> alternatively steam hot then pour over ice for dense cold foam.

### Latte art starter params
- Foam thickness: thin foam (good flow), steady pour rate.
- Spout at cup center, pour point 5-10cm above the surface (higher = pierces and forms a pattern).
- Start with "heart": pour center -> wiggle -> a line finish.
- Common fails: foam too thick / crema too thin / pitcher angle wrong / shaky hands.

### Adjustment
Too bitter -> less coffee / more milk-water or coarser grind; too sweet -> halve the syrup; too sour -> less lemon or switch to medium-dark roast.

## 10. Drip Bag

For: drip bag + mug + hot water

- Dose: 10g (factory); Water: 150g (~1:15); Temp: 90-93C; Time: 2-3 min; Grind: medium (factory)
- Steps: tear & hang on the cup -> bloom with 20-30g 20s -> pour 2-3 stages to 150g -> discard
- Too bitter -> grounds are fixed; add water or lower temp a touch; too sour -> less water or raise temp a touch

## 11. Syphon (Siphon)

For: syphon + spirit/gas burner + cloth/steel filter + stirrer

- Dose: 20g; Water: 300g (~1:15); Temp: ~90-95C after boil; Time: steep 60-90s upper; Grind: medium (raw sugar)
- Steps: heat lower water -> rises to upper -> add grounds & stir -> steep 60-90s -> remove heat -> coffee draws down -> pour
- Too bitter -> shorter steep / gentler stir; too sour -> longer steep / stir more

## 12. Turkish Coffee (Cezve / Ibrik)

For: cezve/ibrik + low heat

- Dose: 10g; Water: 120g (~1:12); Temp: heat to near-boil, do not overflow; Time: 6-8 min; Grind: extra-fine (flour)
- Steps: cold water + grounds + sugar, stir -> low heat -> foam rises, lift off -> repeat 2-3 times -> rest 1 min -> pour with grounds (unfiltered)
- Too bitter -> lower heat / fewer foam cycles; too sour -> cook longer / one more foam cycle

## 13. Flash Brew (Japanese iced)

For: dripper + filter + kettle + iced carafe + ice

- Dose: 20g; Water: hot 200g + ice 80g; Temp: 93-96C; Time: 2:00-3:00; Grind: medium-fine
- Steps: put ice in the carafe -> pour as a hot pour-over -> coffee hits the ice and chills instantly, locking aroma
- Too bitter -> coarser; too sour -> finer

## 14. Vietnamese Phin

For: phin filter + press plate + cup + sweetened condensed milk (optional)

- Dose: 15g; Yield: 100g (~1:7 strong); Temp: 90-94C; Time: 3-5 min drip; Grind: medium-coarse
- Steps: spread condensed milk in cup (optional) -> add grounds & press plate -> bloom 30s -> fill & cover -> drip to strong coffee -> stir
- Too bitter -> loosen press plate / lower temp; too sour -> tighten plate / raise temp

## Important notes
- Any "named-barista recipe" MUST be verified online; do not fabricate numbers (dose/water/temp/time/pressure) from memory. If not found, give universal starter params labeled "general reference."
- Coffee taste is subjective; mantras and params are starting points — your palate is the destination; change ONE variable at a time.
- Beans are themselves a variable: brew a baseline cup with old params first, then adjust.

## Seasonal & environment tweaks
- Summer / warm room: beans age faster; bloom slightly, consider lowering temp 1-2C; body drops a touch.
- Winter / cold room: keep the brew vessel and cup warm; cold equipment drops temp fast and weakens extraction.
- Humidity: high humidity can make grounds clump; break up grounds before brewing (WDT/distribution).
