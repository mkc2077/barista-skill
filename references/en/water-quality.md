# Water quality (the most-overlooked extraction variable)

> A cup of coffee is 98% water. Bad water ruins even the best beans.

## 0. Key parameters

| Parameter | Recommended range | Unit | Notes |
|------|------|------|------|
| **TDS** (total dissolved solids) | 80-150 | ppm | total minerals |
| **General hardness** | 50-175 | ppm CaCO3 | calcium + magnesium |
| **pH** | 6.5-7.5 | - | near neutral |
| **Alkalinity** | 40-75 | ppm CaCO3 | buffers extraction acids |
| **Free chlorine** | 0 | - | must be removed (activated carbon) |
| **Sodium Na** | < 30 | ppm | too high = salty |
| **Chloride Cl** | < 50 | ppm | too high = astringent |

> **Home mantra**: water should have a little "substance" (not pure/distilled), but not be too hard (not untreated hard tap water).

## 1. Recommended solutions

### 1. Commercial / serious enthusiast
- **Reverse osmosis (RO) + remineralizing filter**: precise TDS control; standard in specialty shops.
- Examples: AquaLab, Claris, Brita commercial cartridges, BWT Bestmax, 3M AP2 series.

### 2. Home value
- **Activated-carbon filter jug** (Brita etc.): removes chlorine; limited effect on hardness.
- **Low-mineral bottled water** (e.g. Nongfu Spring, tested ~30-60 TDS, usable but low); for max sweetness & body choose TDS 80-150 mineral water (Kunlun Mountain etc.) or RO+remineralizer; Yibao is near-pure and not ideal.
- **Small RO purifier** (Xiaomi/Haier/Qinyuan etc.): output near pure; add a remineralizer to restore some minerals.

### 3. Direct use
- Hard-water-region tap (scale in the kettle): use a home softener or remineralizer.
- Soft-water-region tap: basically usable after filtration.
- Barreled pure water: not ideal -- lacks minerals, weak/sour extraction.

## 2. Diagnosis scripts (asking the user about water)

**Beginner**:
> "What water do you usually brew with at home? -- (1) straight tap? (2) filtered? (3) pure/distilled? (4) mineral?
> Hard water (kettle scale) extracts unevenly and tastes astringent; pure/distilled is too 'clean' -- under-extracts and tastes sour. Home tip: filtered or low-mineral bottled, NOT pure water."

**Advanced**:
> "TDS and hardness strongly affect extraction. Have you measured your water's TDS? If not, default: activated-carbon filtered or low-TDS bottled (roughly Nongfu-class), target TDS 80-150. Espresso machines on hard water scale -- add a softening/remineralizing cartridge."

## 3. Water impact by brew method

| Method | Water impact | Advice |
|------|------|------|
| Espresso | Large. Scale clogs machines; minerals hit extraction & taste | filtered or low-mineral water mandatory |
| Pour-over | Medium. Directly affects sweet-sour balance & body | filtered or low-mineral |
| Moka pot | Large. Hard water scales | filtered |
| Cold brew / ice drip | Small. Long extraction dilutes mineral differences | ordinary filtered is fine |
| French press | Small | ordinary filtered is fine |

## 4. Testing tools
- **TDS pen**: ~$3-7, gives a numeric reference (it measures total dissolved solids, good or bad).
- **Pro water-test kit**: hardness, pH, alkalinity, chloride etc., ~$5-15.
- **Quick 'good enough' test**: brew one pour-over with the recommended water vs your usual water, same bean; if the former is clearer and more balanced, your water passes.

## 5. Don'ts
- NO pure/distilled/ultra-filtered water (no minerals -> under-extracts, sour or "watery").
- NO untreated hard tap water (scale, astringent).
- NO sparkling/soda water (affects extraction, weird texture).
- NO repeatedly boiled water (low dissolved oxygen, weaker extraction).
- YES filtered or low-mineral bottled; boil fresh each time.

## 6. Pairing with beans
- **Light roast**: use slightly higher-mineral water (TDS 100-150) to lift sweetness & body.
- **Dark roast**: lower-mineral water (TDS 80-120) to avoid over-extracted bitterness.
- Advanced users can buy "recipe water" packs and reconstitute to SCA water standard (TDS 150, hardness 68, alkalinity 40).

## 7. When parameters don't match expectations
If the bean, the grind, and the time are all correct but it still tastes "off" -- ASK ABOUT THE WATER FIRST.
This is the root cause of ~80% of "I followed the guide but it didn't work."
