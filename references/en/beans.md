# Coffee beans: understanding, selecting, storing

Turn "beans" into "how to brew, how to choose, how to store." Left column = beginner plain-language, right = advanced parameters. Reply in the column matching the user's level; don't mix. Pairs with sensory.md and recipes-baseline.md.

## 0. How to read a coffee bag (label reading)

### Beginner (four plain looks)
1. **Roast depth**: dark = black & shiny, bitter/aromatic; light = light brown, fruity & more acidic.
2. **Origin**: Brazil / Colombia / Yunnan / Ethiopia ... different places taste different (Ethiopia often fruity/floral).
3. **Flavor notes**: "nut / chocolate / berry" on the bag is a direction, NOT added flavoring.
4. **Date**: find the "roasted-on" date; the more recent the fresher.

### Advanced (professional dimensions)
- **Origin / Region**: the flavor base
- **Variety**: Typica, Bourbon, Geisha, Caturra, Catimor ...
- **Process**: Washed / Natural / Honey / Anaerobic
- **Altitude**: higher usually = brighter acidity, more delicate
- **Roast**: light / medium / medium-dark / dark
- **Flavor notes / certifications**: descriptors, organic, Rainforest Alliance, etc.

## 1. Picking beans by brew method

| What you brew | Beginner | Advanced |
|------|------|------|
| Espresso (shot / latte) | medium-dark, bold (bitter-aromatic, chocolate); avoid too-sour/light or the shot is sharp | medium-dark blend; target sweetness & body; light single-origin as SOE needs higher temp/finer |
| Pour-over | light-medium is fun; fruit & florals come through; dark works but tends bitter | light-medium single-origin to showcase origin; tune temp to roast |
| French press / AeroPress | medium-dark, full, easy-drinking | medium-dark, forgiving |
| Cold brew / ice drip | dark is aromatic & less sour; or a cheap dark blend | dark or medium-dark; long extraction won't show sharp acid |
| Signature | depends on the pairing: milk drink -> medium-dark; fruity drink -> light & fruity | pick the bean to the pairing |

## 2. Bean-card analysis -> recommended brew (choose the brew to the bean)

When the user posts a bean card or describes the bean, ANALYZE first, then recommend -- don't apply defaults. Match by:

| Bean-card key | Recommended brew | Starter points (beginner / advanced) |
|------|------|------|
| Light single-origin (Ethiopia/Kenya/Geisha, floral/acid) | pour-over (V60/flat-bottom), iced pour-over | medium-fine, 90-93C, 1:15-1:16; showcase acid & aroma, no high temp pushing bitter |
| Medium (balanced, nut/chocolate) | pour-over / French press / AeroPress / espresso all fine | neutral, most forgiving, beginner-friendly |
| Medium-dark/dark blend (bitter, cocoa) | espresso (shot/latte/cappuccino), moka pot, cold brew | espresso 1:2, 88-92C; dark -> coarser to avoid burnt |
| Natural (fruity, full) | pour-over, French press (not too fine) | slightly coarser, lower temp, avoid over-extraction mud |
| Honey (sweet-forward) | pour-over, AeroPress | medium grind, sweetness comes easily |
| Anaerobic / heavy fermentation (intense) | pour-over (controlled) or signature | even extraction, don't over-extract into off-notes |
| Balanced origins (Yunnan/Brazil/Colombia) | match the roast, all work | medium is safest |
| Light SOE (single-origin for espresso) | espresso ristretto / flat white / dirty | SOE light: higher temp, finer, machine tuning; ristretto is stronger and sweeter |

**Bean-card analysis steps**:
- Beginner: look at three things -- roast depth, origin, flavor words (e.g. "berry", "nut") -- then pick a method; variety/process not required.
- Advanced: parse variety/process/altitude/roast, match to a brew & params, and explain WHY.
> Example: user posts "Ethiopia Natural Geisha light roast" -> pour-over / iced pour-over; medium-fine, 90-93C, 1:16; showcase floral/fruit. If they want a signature drink, point them to a light-SOE route rather than a dark blend.

## 3. Bean character -> extraction tuning

| Bean | Beginner (how to tune) | Advanced (how to tune) |
|------|------|------|
| Dark (black, shiny, bitter) | grind **coarser**, water not too hot (~80s-90s C). Mantra: dark roast -> coarser & lower temp | coarser, 88-90C, shorter; avoid burnt-bitter |
| Light (light brown, fruity/acid) | grind **finer**, hotter water (~93C). Mantra: light roast -> finer & higher temp | finer, 93-96C, slightly longer; avoid sharp acid |
| Natural (fruity, full) | a touch coarser, cooler water, avoid mud | slightly coarser, lower temp, prevent over-extracted off-notes |
| Honey (sweet-forward) | brew like a medium, the sweetness comes out | medium params; mind even extraction |
| Anaerobic / heavy fermentation | extract evenly, don't push hard; if weird, coarser & cooler | even distribution, controlled extraction; avoid over-fermented off-notes |
| Very fresh (just roasted) | rest a few days so gas escapes; if in a hurry, brew normally but don't grind too fine | rest 3-7 days to degas; fresh beans extract unevenly, mind distribution |
| Old (1-2 months+) | grind finer, hotter, more grounds if weak | finer, hotter, higher dose; fight flavor loss & under-extraction |

## 4. Freshness & storage

### Beginner
- Fresher is better, but the first days off roast are too gassy -- rest 3-7 days for best results.
- Store sealed, cool, out of the sun; don't refrigerate (moisture/odor), unless storing long-term.
- Once opened, finish within 2-4 weeks.

### Advanced
- **Resting**: light 5-10 days, dark 3-5 days to degas before flavor stabilizes.
- **Peak window**: 2-4 weeks off roast is peak; espresso can run a little longer.
- **Storage**: one-way valve bag / sealed canister, dark & cool (15-25C); long-term (>1 mo) portion and freeze; bring the whole bag to room temp before opening to avoid condensation.
- Humidity is the enemy: damp beans give off-notes and uneven extraction.

## 5. Beginner quick-mantra card (show the user proactively)
> Dark roast -> coarser & lower temp; light roast -> finer & higher temp;
> fresh beans -> rest first; old beans -> finer & warmer.

## 6. Flavor preference -> bean direction (choose beans to taste)

When the user says "I like chocolate" or "I like fruity/sour," map it. Have them picture "what I want it to taste like" FIRST, then pick the bean -- half the trial-and-error gone.

| Desired taste | Beginner (what to buy) | Advanced (origin/process/roast) |
|------|------|------|
| Chocolate, nut, caramel, full body | medium-dark, "bold bitter-aromatic," not too acidic | medium-dark blend; Brazil/Colombia washed; target sweetness & body |
| Floral, fruity, tea-like, clean | light roast, bag says "berry/citrus/jasmine" | light single-origin; Ethiopia/Kenya; natural or honey for aroma |
| Sweet-sour, juicy, explosive | light roast, "tropical fruit/fermented" | light; honey Colombia / natural Ethiopia; anaerobic for intensity |
| Balanced, forgiving, easy | medium roast, "balanced/brown sugar" | medium; Yunnan/Brazil/Colombia; most forgiving, beginner-first |

**Leading scripts**:
- Beginner: "Picture what you like -- thick like chocolate, or clean & fruity? Pick from the table above."
- Advanced: tune by altitude/variety -- higher altitude = brighter acid; Geisha = floral/tea; Bourbon = sweet/clean; blend = stable/balanced.
> Example: "I want something fruity, sweet & sour" -> light single-origin (Ethiopia/Kenya, natural or honey); pour-over reveals it best; avoid medium-dark blends that crush the fruit acid.

## 7. Usage notes
- Beginners: beans always in plain words ("dark/light", "fresh/old", "fruity or not") -- NO roast level / process / resting / peak-window terms (see glossary.md for swaps).
- Advanced: give variety, process, altitude etc. and explain how they jointly affect extraction & flavor.
- Swapping beans is ITSELF a variable -- brew a baseline cup with the old params first to confirm it's the bean, then tune (rules in SKILL.md "iron rules").
