# Sensory & flavor-tuning map

This file turns "how it tastes" into "what to change next." Left column = beginner plain-language, right = advanced parameter. Reply in the column matching the user's experience level; don't mix.

## 0. How to taste coffee (proactively teach the user to describe flavor)

Don't wait to be asked. After giving a recipe or a tweak, actively guide the user to describe the cup.

### Beginner (plain 3-step taste method)
1. **Smell first**: is it aromatic? Like nuts, chocolate, or fruit?
2. **Sip and swirl it in the mouth**: is it sour, bitter, or sweet?
3. **After swallowing**: does the aroma linger in the mouth ("sweet finish")?
> Plain words are fine: "a bit sour, not too bitter, sweet after swallowing." No jargon needed.

### Advanced (six professional tasting dimensions)
Use slurping to aerosolize and capture dry/wet aroma:
- **Aroma**: dry, wet
- **Acidity**: bright / rounded / sharp
- **Sweetness**: obvious / hidden
- **Body**: light / heavy / silky
- **Aftertaste**: length, cleanliness
- **Balance**: are the dimensions in harmony

## 1. Taste problem -> what to change

| How it tastes | Beginner (what to change) | Advanced (what to change) |
|------|------|------|
| Too bitter, burnt, herbal | grind **coarser**; shorten the time. Mantra: bitter->coarser | coarser grind / drop temp 1-2C / shorten time / lower ratio; check over-extraction |
| Too sour, sharp, unripe | grind **finer**; extend the time. Mantra: sour->finer | finer grind / raise temp / extend time / raise ratio; check under-extraction |
| Watery, no flavor, hollow | grind finer, or **more grounds less water** | more grounds / finer / lower ratio (e.g. pour-over 1:15->1:14) |
| Too strong, heavy, muddy | grind coarser, or **more water less grounds** | less grounds / coarser / raise ratio |
| Water runs through too fast, thin | grind **finer** | finer / more dose / check distribution |
| Water stalls, blocked | grind **coarser** | coarser / less dose / check tamp |
| Astringent, dry/scraping | grind coarser, don't make the water too hot | coarser / lower temp / check channeling & over-extraction |
| Smells good, tastes flat | more grounds, grind finer | more dose / finer / confirm temp & freshness |

|------|------|------|
| Bright / sharp acid | prominent lively acidity | under-extraction or light roast |

## 4. SCA flavor wheel (principles & use)

### 4.1 Structure
The wheel is a concentric radial structure, growing outward in layers:
- **Innermost**: the five basic tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami)
- **1st ring**: nine flavor categories
- **2nd ring**: sub-groups
- **Outermost**: ~99 specific descriptors

### 4.2 The "gap distance" principle
- **Adjacent -> tightly related**: a narrow gap between two flavor items means high similarity.
- **Large gap / distance -> weak relation**: a wide gap means weak relation, greater difference.
- **From inner to outer**: toward the outer ring, similarity & relation between flavors weaken.

### 4.3 Correct use
1. **Search from the center outward**: fix a basic taste -> find its category -> narrow to a specific descriptor.
2. **Pair with the sensory lexicon**: SCAA recommends using the wheel with the WCR Sensory Lexicon, where each flavor item has a clear definition and a calibration reference.
3. **As industry shared language**: the wheel's core value is a common language so people across regions and roles can communicate coffee sensory information without barriers.

## 5. Systematic sensory training

### 5.1 Basic taste-recognition training
Per the SCA sensory-foundations standard, the five basic-taste training solutions (per 1L water):

| Basic taste | Solution (dissolve in 1L water) |
|----------|----------------------|
| Sweet | 24g sucrose |
| Sour | 1.2g citric acid |
| Salty | 4g sodium chloride (table salt) |
| Bitter | 0.54g caffeine |
| Umami | 2g MSG (monosodium glutamate) |

**Advanced intensity drill**: make 4 intensity levels per taste (1 weakest, 4 strongest), tiered tasting & blind testing:
1. taste each intensity of sweet-sour-salty on its own
2. same-class same-intensity mixes; same-class different-intensity mixes
3. cross-class different-intensity mixes; feel masking & enhancement between tastes
4. blind tasting -- identify the substances & intensities in mixed solutions

**Coffee acidity special (per CQI Q-Grader)**:
- Citric: citrus acids (lemon, orange)
- Malic: green-apple acid, crisp sweet-sour
- Tartaric: grape acid
- Acetic: vinegar-like, unpleasant acid
- Chlorogenic: decomposes during roast into quinic & caffeic acids

### 5.2 Olfactory memory training

**Key insight**: what we casually call "apple flavor" is not taste but **retronasal olfaction** smelled from the mouth. The "lollipop test" shows it -- pinch your nose and eat a fruit lollipop, you taste only sweet; release your nose and flavor blooms instantly.

**Le Nez du Cafe (36 aromas)**: created by Jean Lenoir (France); SCA certification uses it as a Q-Grader exam item. Grouped by roast chemistry into four clusters:

| Cluster | Roast stage | Aroma character | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|---------|
| Enzymatic | light roast | floral, fruity, herbal | rose, coffee blossom, lemon, apricot, apple |
| Sugar browning | medium roast | nutty, caramel, cocoa | honey, caramel, dark chocolate, roasted almond, roasted hazelnut |
| Dry distillation | dark roast | resinous, spicy, burnt | clove, pepper, tobacco, smoky |
| Taints | - | unpleasant defect | earthy, potato, green bean, rubber |

**Daily olfactory memory**: build a library from everyday foods -- citrus/berry/peach; rose/jasmine; almond/hazelnut; cinnamon/clove/vanilla. Core: taste widely, remember everyday scents.

### 5.3 Comparative tasting

| Drill | What | Progression |
|---------|------|---------|
| Triangulation | 3 cups, 2 same, find the odd | different origin -> process -> roast -> same-farm different batch |
| Origin compare | contrast origins (Africa / C. America / Asia) | broad region first, then neighbors |
| Defect cupping | spot off-flavors (earthy, musty, over-fermented) | obvious -> subtle |
| Roast compare | same bean, different roasts | understand roast impact |

### 5.4 Personal coffee olfactory & taste memory bank
1. **Taste base**: sour-sweet-salty-bitter-umami standard solutions, 4 intensity levels.
2. **Retronasal bank**: lollipop & fresh-fruit blind tests; link retronasal olfaction to taste.
3. **Dry/wet aroma bank**: 36 aromas / T100 system + everyday foods.
4. **Coffee mapping**: cup across origins/processes/roasts, record and locate vs the wheel.
5. **Temperature bank**: record the same cup at hot / medium / cool.
6. **Sensory hygiene**: avoid smoke, alcohol, very spicy food; keep senses sharp.
