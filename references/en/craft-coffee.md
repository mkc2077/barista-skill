# Craft / signature coffee (a standalone major category)

> Craft coffee is a **standalone major category** in this skill, not an extension of milk drinks. It has its own full coffee-base extraction specs, tea-base specs, homemade-adjunct specs, store-bought ingredient list, and SOP (standard operating procedure).
> Iron rule: This file gives "framework + verified method names and shop index." Any specific grams / extraction params / current shop recipe MUST be re-verified online against the shop/blogger's current public version before you write it into a reply, with a source link + retrieval date. Craft drinks iterate fast and are often seasonal; shop menus change. Always defer to the current shop menu / official account.

## 1. What is craft coffee / how to classify

Craft = **coffee base + adjuncts + flavor/presentation** forming a shop/blog signature. Two base families:

| Base family | Coffee liquor source | Typical |
|-------------|----------------------|---------|
| **Espresso base** | espresso / ristretto | cocoa latte, SOE Dirty, Siyou limited Dirty |
| **Infusion base** | pour-over / cold brew / flash brew | jasmine cold brew, mango craft, ALOHA |

> Key: the same craft's extraction spec must stay constant to reproduce. Online shop craft recipes often only say "coffee liquor" — **you must complete the extraction spec yourself**.

## 2. Coffee base extraction specs (mandatory in every recipe)

Every craft recipe must state the base extraction spec (one or more) in the first line with full params. This is what makes the SOP reproducible.

### A. Mid-dark roast espresso (classic / milk base)
- Use: milk-base craft, cocoa/nut/chocolate thick crafts. Needs to tame bitterness, needs thick crema to support foam and layering.
- Beans: medium-dark blend (Colombia/Brazil/Guatemala etc.), sweet-thick.
- Spec (generic starter; verify shop beans): 18g / 36g out (1:2) / 92–94C / 9 bar / 25–30s.
- Variants: stretch to 1:2.5 (lungo) for a lighter smoother base; stop before 1:2 to take only the middle cut when adjuncts add sweetness.

### B. Mid-light SOE ristretto (Single Origin Espresso, short cut)
- Use: "this bean itself" crafts — fruity SOE flat white, light-roast Dirty, bright-acidity clear-drink crafts.
- Beans: mid-light single origin (Ethiopia washed/natural, Kenya, Geisha etc.); want floral/fruit acidity.
- Spec: 18–20g / 18–27g out (1:1–1:1.5 ristretto) / 93–95C / 9 bar / 20–25s. **Take only the front-mid cut; avoid the bitter tail.**
- Variants: cleaner -> stop at 1:1 (18g/18g) including crema; fuller -> pull to 1:1.5.
- Note: light-roast SOE crema is thinner (verified via qianjie test — same params, light SOE crema noticeably less than medium/dark), weaker layering, adjust presentation.

### C. Pour-over base
- Use: clear/tea-coffee crafts, clean fruit acid as base; tropical/floral crafts.
- Spec (generic golden-cup starter): 15–20g / 225–300g water (1:15–1:16) / 90–92C / 2:00–2:45. Dripper by flavor goal (V60 cone = bright acidity; Kalita wave = round body; see champion-brewing section 2).
- Variants: iced pour-over — push to 1:8–1:10, brew a concentrate that hits ice (ice weight ~= water). See recipes-baseline iced pour-over.

### D. Cold brew base
- Use: low acid, smooth, fruit/tea crafts; jasmine cold brew, ALOHA.
- Spec: 1:8–1:12 cold steep / 12–24h refrigerated / coarse grind. Filter, dilute to 1:15 to drink.
- Variants: Dutch ice drip — 1:10–1:15, 1 drop/s ice water, 6–12h; softer body, more winey.

> When writing a craft recipe, pick one of the four and write it fully: bean+roast / dose+yield+ratio / temp+pressure / time.

## 3. Tea base specs (mandatory when a craft includes tea)

Many shop crafts use **tea** as a second base (jasmine latte, oolong+coffee, milk-tea craft). Tea base and coffee base are two separate SOPs; **spell tea base out separately**.

| Tea | Spec (generic starter; verify shop's current) | Use |
|-----|-----------------------------------------------|-----|
| Green/jasmine | 1:50–1:80 / 80–85C / 1.5–2.5min | jasmine cold brew, clear crafts |
| Oolong (tieguanyin/da hong pao/dancong) | 1:40–1:60 / 90–95C / 1.5–3min (use 2nd steep mainly) | milk-tea crafts, flavored milk tea |
| Black (Ceylon/Assam/Earl Grey) | 1:40–1:60 / 92–95C / 2–4min | classic milk tea, Earl Grey coffee |
| Cold-brew tea | 1:100 / cold brew 4–8h | low-tannin base, fruit-tea crafts |
| Tea concentrate | 1:20 short strong brew -> use as base syrup | efficient for craft |

> Tea base and coffee liquor are **extracted separately, weighed separately**, assembled in SOP. Mind temperature matching when both warm — avoid pouring hot coffee into cold tea stock (flavor break).

## 4. Homemade syrup / adjunct specs (must give full SOP)

Shop craft adjuncts are mostly **homemade**, not off-the-shelf — syrups, purees, marmalades. Reproducing needs a **homemade SOP**, never "syrup to taste."

### Universal homemade sugar syrup SOP
- Ratio: sugar:water = 1:1 (heavy syrup) or 1:2 (light syrup).
- Method: cold water in pot -> add sugar, stir -> low heat until dissolved (don't boil) -> off heat -> cool -> bottle sterile -> fridge <=2 weeks.
- Flavor variants:
  - Vanilla: add 1 split vanilla bean (or 1 tsp extract), simmer 10min, steep 1h, strain.
  - Caramel: dry-cook sugar to amber (~170C) -> slowly add hot water off-heat (anti-splash) -> stir, cool.
  - Ginger: 50ml water + 30g ginger slices simmer 10min -> add 50g sugar, dissolve, strain.
  - Cinnamon/star-anise: add 1 stick or 2 stars to base syrup, steep 2h.

### Puree / jam SOP (mango puree, berry jam)
- Mango puree: fresh mango 200g + sugar 20g + lemon juice 5ml -> blend -> simmer thick -> fridge.
- Berry jam: berries 200g + sugar 40g + lemon 10ml -> low simmer 10–15min until pectin sets -> fridge.
- Cascara syrup: cascara 50g + water 250ml + sugar 100g -> simmer 10min -> steep 30min -> strain.

### Oils / alcohols
- Orange peel oil: use only colored part; quick-torch before use to tame bitterness.
- Liqueurs (coffee liqueur, orange, rum): 5–15ml per cup, add at the end; mark "contains alcohol, adults only, not for pregnancy."

## 5. Store-bought ingredient list (juice, coconut water, sparkling, etc.)

These are **not homemade**, but purchase points and brand direction must be noted:

| Adjunct | Purchase points (generic) | Note |
|---------|--------------------------|------|
| Coconut water | 100% coconut water, no added sugar | Sweetness varies by brand; recipe must note brand or calibrate per sweetness |
| Sparkling water | high carbonation, low sodium (e.g. San Pellegrino, Gerolsteiner) | Don't let sit in cup, lose gas |
| Fresh juice | fresh-squeezed best; common — orange/lemon/passionfruit/grapefruit | Mark "fresh" or "100% NFC" |
| Milk / plant milk | whole milk = thick foam; oat milk needs barista-version for foam | Brand varies by oil/sweetness |
| Maple / honey | flavor syrup substitutes | Lower sweetness than white sugar; calibrate |
| Cocoa / matcha powder | food-grade, unsweetened raw powder | Sift matcha first |

> When advising, **homemade in section 4, store-bought in section 5** — don't mix. List both clearly per craft.

## 6. Craft SOP framework (every craft must give full steps)

Craft SOP ≠ just a recipe. The complete structure below must be filled in when reproducing:

```markdown
[Craft SOP template]
Craft name: {name} (from {shop/blogger}, {city}, menu as of {YYYY-MM-DD})

1. Coffee base (mandatory)
   - Type: {A mid-dark espresso / B SOE ristretto / C pour-over / D cold brew}
   - Beans: {origin/roast/blend or SOE}
   - Params: dose {g} / yield or total water {g} ({ratio}) / temp {C} / pressure {bar} / time {s}
   - Notes: {mid cut only / crema or not / pre-diluted}

2. Tea base (mandatory if tea, else "none")
   - Tea + ratio + temp + time
   - Cold-brewed? second steep?

3. Homemade adjuncts (each item, in build order, with SOP)
   - Adjunct1: {name} {g} — SOP (which section-4 variant)
   - Adjunct2: ...

4. Store-bought adjuncts (each, brand/spec direction)
   - coconut water {g} / sparkling {g} / fresh OJ {g} / ...

5. Glass & ice
   - Glass: {360ml glass / 300ml highball / 250ml ceramic latte}
   - Ice: {no ice / regular 100g / large cube 80g / crushed}

6. Build SOP (ordered, with mnemonics)
   1) {first in cup}       e.g. syrup {g} to bottom
   2) {second in cup}      e.g. ice to 80%
   3) {third in cup}       e.g. juice down the ice wall (not to bottom)
   4) {fourth in cup}      e.g. coffee liquor poured slowly on top (hold layer)
   5) {finish}             e.g. flamed orange-peel oil + stir stick tilted
   Mnemonic: "{phrase}-{phrase}"

7. Presentation & drinking notes
   - Stir? sip in stages? layer order
   - Best drinking window: within X minutes
   - If alcoholic, mark it

8. Source
   - [{title}]({url}), retrieved {YYYY-MM-DD}
   - Reproducing: "menu as of this date"; shops rotate
```

## 8. Espresso craft universal points (read before refining)

- Beans: milk craft uses medium-dark blend; bean-forward (SOE flat white, craft) uses light-roast SOE. See [recipes-baseline.md](recipes-baseline.md) section 9.
- Ingredient order: syrup/puree first, espresso next, then milk/foam/ice — avoid breaking the crema.
- Iced: weigh ice; hot extraction over ice dilutes instantly, especially in craft.
- Balance: when adjuncts add sweetness, base espresso should be stronger (e.g. ristretto pull).
- Presentation: layers from density difference (syrup bottom, milk middle, foam top); Dirty is the classic no-stir layer.

## 10. Common pitfalls

- Don't copy a shop recipe without asking about the beans — shop blends are custom; swap to a similar roast at home.
- Don't give a recipe without an extraction spec — no "mid-dark 18g/36g" or "SOE ristretto 18g/22g" means the craft won't reproduce.
- Don't blur homemade vs store-bought adjuncts — "add syrup" tells nobody what/which/how-much.
- Don't give a recipe without steps — craft lives on build order and temperature; skipping the SOP fails.
- Don't let heavy ingredients bury the coffee — craft is balance; coffee must remain, not sugar water.
- Don't fabricate grams/ratios from a name — every number must come from online verification with source.
- Don't blur seasonal vs permanent — many signatures are seasonal; label "as of (date) menu."
- Do copy the framework (base + adjunct categories + tea) first, then tune ratios and SOP to your palate.
